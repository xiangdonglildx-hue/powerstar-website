Humidity Levels Guide

✅ Optimal: 30-50% Humidity

Ideal for health, comfort, and home protection. No mold, no dry skin, minimal allergens.

⚠️ Too Dry: Below 30%

Causes dry skin, static electricity, increased virus transmission, and respiratory irritation.

❌ Too Humid: Above 50%

Promotes mold growth, dust mites, musty odors, and can damage furniture and electronics.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the ideal indoor humidity level?

A: The ideal indoor humidity level is between 30-50%. Below 30% can cause dry skin, respiratory issues, and static electricity. Above 50% promotes mold growth, dust mites, and musty odors. Our hygrometer app helps you maintain this optimal range.

Q: Does my phone have a humidity sensor?

A: Many modern Android phones include humidity sensors. If your phone doesn't have one, the app estimates humidity using local weather data combined with indoor temperature readings. Samsung Galaxy S4 and newer often include this sensor.

Q: Why should I monitor humidity at home?

A: Proper humidity levels prevent health issues and protect your home. Low humidity causes dry skin, throat irritation, and increases virus transmission. High humidity leads to mold, allergens, and can damage furniture, electronics, and wooden structures.
